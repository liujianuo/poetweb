For V. Lessons for the Road For V.
Sappy pieces (only the presentable ones)
I like to think that in the quadrillions of words that man has written, that
Love is the most potent, its simple yet inexplicably intricate nature being the
One greatest folly, and yet also the one greatest joy of man. In peace and
Violence, both the same, it plays its part as the puppetmaster, acting its
Ethereal play, us adherents, its dolls, playing the tragic roles of its
Youthful and sadistic imagination.
Of how it decides upon its targets, I know naught, yet its pounding,
Unadulterated vigour, which fills my heart, nerve, and sinew, with
Vivacious might that rivals the light of day, brings my mind to sing
In ways previously unknown.
Vivid images fill my thoughts with possibilities the world beholds, yet
In each of them the same brilliant pair of eyes alight seem to see these same
Absurd fantasies as the reality of the future. All I ask is that this
Nurturing pair of eyes are yours.
Gone are the days of blue skies and birds chirping
On the branch of the arbutus, standing high and tall
Over the newly sprouted dandelion and weed,
Decorating the field, which blends into the
Natural, rolling hills that meet the sky
In a kaleidescope of intersections.
Gone is the pounding summer sun and the
Heat (oh how it raged) that drove even
The most resilient of us into insomnia in the night.
Bearing the harvest's full wreath of pastoral wealth,
In great regret, Father Time passes us by.
Gone is Gaia's loving glow in the harsh frigid cold, leaving
Behind only ourselves to keep company and dutifully
Observe the transient state of each snowflake, for as
You turn your attention elsewhere, even if only for a
Brisk moment, it's gone, leaving behind only an
Imprint of what was just there.
Gone is the color from the land, leaving only the
Dreary shades of white and gray that dot the landscape
And fuse into dots and smears of light and dark with the
Yellow of warm, hospitable light breaking the sheet of
Tender, suffocating angst that filled the air.
My dear, fret not over the bleak frigid wild, but
Rest assured that the warmth of others will keep the light ablaze.
Words are not enough to explain to you
What blossoms deep within me
As your eyes stare deep into mine.
Words will not suffice to describe the feeling
Of a brisk breeze brushing past
Long, strands of hair fluttering,
Carefree, like floating on air.

Words can not express the hope
That one day, perhaps we can be forever
Time intertwined two threads to one.
That one day, we will stay together
No matter the circumstances. No matter the strife.

So I drew a little picture

Violet sky.
The last rays of sun retreat behind the horizon,
and create a gradient, slowly retreating back
into the dark, abyssal zenith

All of dusk's brilliance has long since faded
the hues of orange, purple and pink all
gone away, only the persistent blue remaining
and covering the entire sky in its dull glow.

The glow of love, although it appears in passionate
colors, hues, and vivid flashes, in this muted form
should be held in no less esteem to its explosive counterpart,
the lustrous blue no less beautiful than the brilliant warm tone.

Lie back, and let your eyes drift from light to dark.
As the stars peep out, and start to shimmer from their inky veil.
And let your mind wander, find infinite possibilities and futures,
like the infinite worlds that exist beyond your gaze, into the universe.
