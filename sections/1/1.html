Lessons for the Road Lessons for the Road For V.
Random sonnet-like pieces written during math periods. Most of them take lines that DrS has said.
Gray smog, what fills the sky with hazy blight.
Surround our town with barren dust-filled silt.
A screen, desires kept from godly sight
Lest He condemn the devil's lair we've built.

Make haste, create our patched seal of ash.
Conceal our sin from nature's loving arms.
The grovelling men who murder unabashed
The life we lived before without alarm.

The dream we had, pursued in primal state,
Has given way for greed and lust unchecked.
Yet hurry not away from human fate,
But revel in the wrongs we did create.

The ruin of our deeds in world's decay
Shall never see the open light of day.
Silence is the sacred music of gods,
Not love as some before me have described.
Some find themselves eternally at odds
To find but just a love that felt contrived.

Mortal muses may shriek in their shrill groans,
Bleed the deaf 'till they have been forsaken.
Compare them not to ethereal tones
In heavenly chords, cadence unbroken.

Silent loyalty is the sacred pact
That holds sinners in just and moral right.
And although love gives us excited act,
It's the hidden that leads us to the light.

Love may plague and prick the lost and broken,
Held from secrets found in the unspoken.
Love is not the sudden flutter of hearts,
Nor the locked eyes of strangers across halls.
Not extravagant displays of fine art,
And not intimacy heard through the walls.

Love is not attraction between lovers,
But the dedication and sacraftice.
Adoration is but under cover,
The vows you made, which carry you through strife.

So be not amused by the heart's folly
And little muses, but rather grounded.
Find love in the budding water lillies
and cherish the time you've been allotted.

Love is the vow to stay 'till the last breath.
Never apart, in this world or the next.
Wilted flowers are just as beautiful
As ones picked fresh from the morning garden.
Winter's harvest may be just as bountiful
When blizzard's chill have the frost roots harden

Broken beauty may be just as cherished,
Held as dearly as Providence's gift.
And imperfection hold equal merit
As flawless bow strokes, lines at perfect lift.

There's a different beauty I hold in hope,
One which common man and art abandoned.
Taken away for plastic, I must cope
And wish that days would take on this vision.

Wilted flowers are just as beautiful,
While man's obsession with it, unfaithful.
How do I explain to you quite gently
The unfortunate mishaps in the world.
How do I show to you the sentiment
Of asymptotes, never to reach the goal.

How do I demonstrate the attraction
Of opposites, set apart in space.
Even all the little abberation
That set coincidental paths away.

How do I demonstrate the affection
Needed to bring impossibles so close.
The affinity of life's direction
To bring together those who were once foes.

Must I demonstrate to you the feeling:
Knowing the impossible, yet dreaming?
Dwell not on the promises that were made,
And mope, stay stagnant, hung up on the past.
Dwell not on what the words were that she said.
But look anew, true love would always last.

What you started will never be finished,
So why believe in the impossible?
What you pledged will never be completed,
So do not chase what will never be yours.

What once was may never return again,
When winter's barren breeze freeze it over.
Time and experience may leave like sand,
Slipping through the cracks in desert's expanse.

Yet dwell not on abandoned promises,
And be left behind as time progresses.
